# References

papers are checked in (arXiv preprints, freely available). Reviews are listed with links
rather than checked in.

## SU(N) and higher genus (the curves this repo formalizes)

- Klemm, Lerche, Yankielowicz, Theisen, *Simple singularities and N=2 supersymmetric Yang–Mills theory*, [hep-th/9411048](https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-th/9411048).
- Argyres, Faraggi, *The vacuum structure and spectrum of N=2 supersymmetric SU(N) gauge theory*, [hep-th/9411057](https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-th/9411057).

These give the hyperelliptic curve `y² = P_N(x)² − Λ^{2N}` (genus `N−1`) used in
`README.md`.

## Pedagogical reviews

- Tachikawa, *N=2 supersymmetric dynamics for pedestrians*, [arXiv:1312.2684](https://arxiv.org/abs/1312.2684) — modern; covers the `SU(2)` SW solution through **Argyres–Douglas CFTs and Gaiotto dualities** (directly relevant to Phase 3).
- Lerche, *Introduction to Seiberg–Witten theory and its stringy origin*, [hep-th/9611190](https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-th/9611190).
- Bilal, *Duality in N=2 SUSY SU(2) Yang–Mills theory: a pedagogical introduction*, [hep-th/9601007](https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-th/9601007).
- Álvarez-Gaumé, Hassan, *Introduction to S-duality in N=2 SUSY gauge theories*, [hep-th/9701069](https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-th/9701069).

*(No dedicated David Tong review of Seiberg–Witten exists; his [Gauge Theory](http://www.damtp.cam.ac.uk/user/tong/gaugetheory.html) and solitons/SUSY lecture notes cover adjacent non-perturbative dynamics — monopoles, instantons — but not the SW solution itself.)*

A self-contained overview oriented toward the formalization is in
[`../intro-to-the-solution.md`](../intro-to-the-solution.md).
