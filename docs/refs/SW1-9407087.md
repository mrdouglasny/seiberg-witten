# Monopole Condensation, and Confinement in N=2 Supersymmetric Yang–Mills Theory

- **Authors:** N. Seiberg, E. Witten
- **arXiv:** [hep-th/9407087](https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-th/9407087)
- **Journal:** Nucl. Phys. **B426** (1994) 19–52; Erratum *ibid.* **B430** (1994) 485
- **PDF:** [`SW1-9407087.pdf`](SW1-9407087.pdf)

The foundational paper. It determines the **exact** low-energy effective action of pure
`N=2` `SU(2)` super Yang–Mills on its Coulomb branch — the branch parametrized by
`u = ⟨Tr φ²⟩`. The low-energy theory is an abelian `N=2` gauge theory fixed by a single
holomorphic prepotential `F(a)`, with effective coupling `τ(a) = F''(a)`. The problem is
that `F` receives a one-loop perturbative piece **plus an infinite series of instanton
corrections**, and is not computable directly.

The solution comes from **holomorphy + duality + the singularity structure**. The
`u`-plane has three singularities: weak coupling at `u→∞` (monodromy fixed by the
one-loop beta function), and two strong-coupling points at `u = ±Λ²` where a **monopole**
(resp. **dyon**) becomes massless. Consistency of the `SL(2,ℤ)` monodromies
(`M_∞ = M_{Λ²} M_{−Λ²}`) together with holomorphy uniquely determines `(a_D, a)` as the
**periods of a meromorphic one-form on a family of elliptic curves** — the Seiberg–Witten
curve. Expanding the periods at weak coupling reproduces the one-loop + instanton series
(later matched, order by order, by Nekrasov's instanton partition function).

Physically, the massless monopole at `u = Λ²`, once `N=2` is broken to `N=1`,
**condenses** — a dual Meissner effect that produces **confinement** of electric charge.

**Relevance to this repo.** This is the headline case we formalize: `SU(2)` is the
**genus-1** specialization, reachable axiom-free from `jacobian-challenge`'s elliptic
period witness. The period geometry, special-Kähler structure (`τ = τᵀ`, `Im τ ≻ 0`), and
the relation `a_D = ∂F/∂a`, `τ = ∂²F/∂a²` are exactly Phases 0–2. See also
[`../foundations-for-physical-reasoning.md`](../foundations-for-physical-reasoning.md) for
how the *physical* inputs (holomorphy, singularity structure, duality, asymptotic
matching) sit as codified axioms above the proved period geometry.
