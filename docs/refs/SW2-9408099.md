# Monopoles, Duality and Chiral Symmetry Breaking in N=2 Supersymmetric QCD

- **Authors:** N. Seiberg, E. Witten
- **arXiv:** [hep-th/9408099](https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-th/9408099)
- **Journal:** Nucl. Phys. **B431** (1994) 484–550
- **PDF:** [`SW2-9408099.pdf`](SW2-9408099.pdf)

The sequel, adding **matter**. It extends the `SU(2)` solution to `N=2` SQCD with
`N_f ≤ 4` hypermultiplet flavors of bare mass `m_i`. For each `N_f` it gives the explicit
Seiberg–Witten curve and one-form; the bare masses enter as **residues of a meromorphic
Seiberg–Witten differential**, so the periods `a, a_D` acquire mass-dependent pieces and
the BPS mass formula gains flavor (quark-number) charges.

The paper works out the singularity structure on each Coulomb branch, the patterns of
**chiral symmetry breaking**, and the BPS spectra, including how massless monopoles/dyons
and quarks appear at special points. The `N_f = 4` theory is **superconformal** (vanishing
beta function) and exhibits an exact `SL(2,ℤ)` electric–magnetic duality acting together
with the flavor and modular structure.

**Relevance to this repo.** This supplies the **mass / meromorphic-period layer** that
Phase 2 of [`../../PLAN.md`](../../PLAN.md) needs: a meromorphic `λ_SW` whose residues are
the masses, on top of the holomorphic period geometry. It is also the physical origin of
the Argyres–Douglas points (colliding singularities) flagged for Phase 3, and the
`N_f = 4` finite theory is the cleanest duality test case.
